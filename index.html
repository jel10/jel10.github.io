Frequently Asked Questions

What dental insurance does ZoomRays take?

We accept all PPO Dental Insurance.

What is ZoomRays and how does it work?

  • Do you have a dental emergency?
  • Do you need a second opinion?
  • Do you want to consult with a dentist but don’t have the time to go to an office?
Simply call ZoomRays and one of our friendly certified dental technicians will arrange to come to your home or workplace at your convenience, and take state of the art digital X-Rays and photos of your teeth. These digital images will then be securely sent to a licensed ZoomRays dentist who will then examine them and write you a detailed report via E-mail within 24 hours.

Wait wait wait... I thought only dental offices have X-rays and other imaging equipment?

Due to recent advances in technology, X-rays and other imaging equipment are now smaller and more portable than ever. ZoomRays uses the exact same state-of-the-art equipment that are used in real dental offices every day; the only difference is, we have our technicians come to you at your time and place of convenience so you don’t have to deal with the hassle of going to the dental office.

Is ZoomRays Safe?

  • All ZoomRays dental technicians have valid and current Radiation Safety Certificates from the Dental Board of the respective states in which they work.
  • All ZoomRays dental technicians maintain current certificates in infection control as well as first aid and CPR.
  • All ZoomRays dental technicians work under the general supervision of a licensed dentist who are just a phone call away.
  • All ZoomRays equipment are certified for use in actual dental offices.
  • All of your personal information and data are secured and encrypted and meets all HIPAA requirements; we will never share your personal information or data with anyone without your permission.
  • Notice: ZoomRays would not be safe for women who are or may be pregnant. Please inform us immediately if you are or may be pregnant.

How do I know if I need X-Rays? Would photos be enough?

While certain dental conditions can be visually seen with photos, many other problems require X-Rays to properly diagnose. Think of X-Rays for dentists as radars are for pilots: just as you wouldn’t want your pilot to fly without their radar, dentists cannot properly diagnose or treat many dental conditions without X-Rays! Studies have shown that the amount of radiation exposure in a full-mouth series of dental X-Rays is equivalent to flying from California to New York on a commercial plane. Rest assured that all ZoomRays equipment are calibrated and maintained according to all relevant regulations. If you do not wish to take the full set of X-Rays or any X-Rays at all, please inform our technicians. We will not take any images without your consent; however, please be advised that the dentist’s report may therefore be much less detailed.

How much does ZoomRays really cost?

For those without dental insurance, our pricing is simply what is listed above. No hidden fees. No add-on costs. If you have PPO dental insurance, ZoomRays' services may be covered up to 100%. Please call and we will verify your insurance eligibility for you. If there are any out-of-pocket expenses, we will inform you ahead of time so that there are never any surprises. In addition, that $99 may be eligible for a credit towards future treatment at one of our partner dental offices local to you. Please call for details.

OK, so the report says I need treatment, now what?

Unfortunately, it is not yet technologically feasible for the dentist to do actual treatment outside of the dental office setting. ZoomRays is designed to help patients at least get an idea of the amount and type of dental treatments he or she may need, as well as the urgency of treatment. We encourage all patients to properly follow up with appropriate dental treatment. ZoomRays can, at your direction, forward your images to any dental office of your choice. Alternatively, we would be happy to refer you to one of our ZoomRays partner dental offices local to you. For cash ZoomRays patients, you may receive credit towards treatment at one of our partner dental offices.
